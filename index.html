Physics

Physics is a branch of science that explores the fundamental principles governing the universe, focusing on how matter, energy, space, and time interact. It spans topics from the motion of everyday objects to the behavior of subatomic particles and the nature of black holes. Key areas within physics include classical mechanics, which deals with motion and forces; electromagnetism, which explains electric and magnetic fields; thermodynamics, which studies heat, energy, and work; quantum mechanics, which uncovers the strange behaviors of particles at the smallest scales; and relativity, which redefines our understanding of space and time.

Chemistry

Chemistry is the scientific study of matter, its properties, composition, structure, and the changes it undergoes during chemical reactions. It explores the interactions between atoms and molecules, which are the building blocks of matter. Chemistry is often referred to as the "central science" because it connects and overlaps with various scientific disciplines such as physics, biology, environmental science, and medicine.

Mathematics

Mathematics is the abstract science of numbers, quantity, structure, space, and change. It is a foundational discipline that explores patterns, relationships, and logical reasoning. Mathematics provides the tools and language to understand and model the natural world, solve problems, and make informed decisions. Mathematics is fundamental to various fields, including science, engineering, economics, and technology. It provides the framework for critical thinking, problem-solving, and innovation in many aspects of life and work.

Mechanics

Mechanics is a fundamental branch of physics that focuses on the study of motion, forces, and the behavior of physical objects. It explores how objects move, the forces that act upon them, and the principles governing their interactions. Mechanics is broadly divided into classical mechanics and quantum mechanics. Classical mechanics deals with the motion of everyday objects, ranging from projectiles to machines, based on Newton's laws of motion. It includes the study of kinematics, which describes motion without considering its causes, dynamics, which analyzes the forces behind motion, and statics, which examines systems in equilibrium.

Basic Electrical Engineering

Basic Electrical Engineering provides a foundational understanding of the principles that govern electrical systems. It covers the analysis of electric circuits, focusing on components like resistors, capacitors, and inductors, and introduces key laws such as Ohm's Law and Kirchhoff's Laws for circuit analysis. The course explores the differences between alternating current (AC) and direct current (DC) systems, their generation, and practical applications. It also delves into power generation, transmission, and the basics of electromagnetism, which are essential for understanding transformers and motors.
